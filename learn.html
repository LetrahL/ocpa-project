Why Exercise Matters

Exercise plays a vital role in both physical and mental health — and it’s especially important for healthy aging. Regular activity improves cardiovascular fitness, builds strength, and enhances flexibility. It also lowers the risk of chronic conditions like obesity, diabetes, and heart disease. It also boosts mood, reduces stress, and contributes to a greater sense of well-being.

Tips for Beginners

  • Start small/slow: Begin with short sessions and gradually increase duration and intensity.
  • Be consistent: Small steps over time lead to results.
  • Find activities you enjoy: Choose exercises that you look forward to doing.
  • Set realistic goals: Focus on what works for you.
  • Listen to your body: Rest when needed and avoid pushing through pain.
  • Seek Guidance: Find trusted, credible sources - there's a lot of misinformation in the fitness community, so be mindful.

Types of Exercise

  • Cardio: Improves heart health and endurance. Examples: running, swimming, cycling.
  • Strength Training: Builds muscle and bone density. Examples: weightlifting, bodyweight exercises.
  • Flexibility: Increases mobility and reduces injury. Examples: yoga, stretching.

Stay Motivated

  • Track your progress.
  • Mix up your workouts.
  • Remind yourself why you started.
  • Celebrate small wins.
  • Find a workout buddy.
  • Join a class or group.
  • Set challenges for yourself.
  • Use social media for support.
  • Stay positive.
  • Reward yourself.
  • Focus on the process, not just the results.
  • Remember that everyone has off days.
